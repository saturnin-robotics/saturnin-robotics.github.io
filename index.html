Research

PhD Thesis · LS2N, École Centrale de Nantes · Ongoing

Cooperative Dynamic Maps for Autonomous Vehicles — A World Model Approach

My thesis investigates how fleets of autonomous vehicles can collaboratively construct and share dynamic, predictive maps of their environment. The central contribution is a framework grounded in world models: learned latent representations that enable vehicles to reason about future states, occlusions, and multi-agent interactions beyond the range of local sensors.

This involves designing scalable V2X communication protocols, uncertainty-aware map merging, and efficient inference architectures deployable on resource-constrained onboard computers.
